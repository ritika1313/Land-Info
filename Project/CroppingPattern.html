üìå What are Cropping Patterns?

Cropping patterns refer to the way crops are cultivated on farmland over time and space. They are influenced by climate, soil type, availability of irrigation, and socio-economic conditions. Understanding cropping patterns is crucial to improving agricultural productivity and sustainability.

Monocropping

Growing the same crop on the same land every year (e.g., wheat in Punjab). It's easy to manage but can deplete soil nutrients.

Mixed Cropping

Two or more crops are grown together (e.g., wheat + gram). Helps reduce the risk of crop failure.

Crop Rotation

Different crops are grown in succession (e.g., rice followed by pulses). Improves soil health and reduces pests.

Intercropping

Growing two or more crops simultaneously in the same field in a specific row pattern. Enhances land use efficiency.
